Let's learn about what neocolonialism is, and how it affects our culture.

What is Neocolonialism and how does it affect us?

Colonialism is the act of a more powerful country enacting physical occupation and control over a less powerful country and influencing it in political, economic, and cultural ways that follow the desires of the occupant.


Neocolonialism however is the practice of larger, influencial, developed countries continuing to influence and control less powerful countries in economic, cultural, and political aspects. However, it is done without direct political or territorial control.


This affects countries like the Philippines when first world countries like the United States expresses power over our society in indirect ways. From the restaurants and businesses that dominate our economy to the political figures and beliefs that influence our government.



The Importance of Filipino Culture

Maintaining appreciation and love for our culture is vital in this day and age as more and more cultures come together. The unification of the world and exchange of cultures is a positive change, however we must remember not to lose sight of our own identity.


The Philippines has been colonized and occupied by 3 different first world countries and has changed significantly as a result, and global trends influence what is popular in the modern day.


These can result in the Filipino population conforming to or following the ideals of other countries and showing more support for them rather than the Philippines. If this continues, the history and culture of the Philippines would continue to diminish until it is forgotten and our identity as Filipinos fades away. That is why it is vital for us to continue to show appreciation and love towards our native culture!


The Beauty of Filipino Culture

The Philippines has many wonders to offer and be appreciated, from beautiful animals and locations to amazing festivals and foods. From cultural traditions like Kalinga batok tattoos to the breathtaking animals like the Philippine eagle, there is so much beauty in our culture that we have to preserve and keep alive.
